Bass Strait
Grandstand

Reserved Seating

Located on the outfield at the lowest point on the Circuit and backed by the coastline’s dramatic undulating terrain and cliffs, the Bass Strait Grandstand provides one of racing’s most unique spectator viewing experiences.

The views from the Bass Strait Grandstand regularly have fans holding their breath as the bikes careen flat out in fifth through the short straight out of Turn 2 before sweeping through Stoner Corner and into the newly named Miller Corner (Turn 4) - one of the Circuit’s prime overtaking hotspots and location of Jack Miller’s heartbreaking crash in 2022.

With a super-screen positioned across the track and with bar and food outlets conveniently located nearby, the Bass Strait Grandstand is an ideal spot for motorsport connoisseurs to enjoy the fast-flowing intricacies of the iconic Phillip Island Grand Prix Circuit.

Lukey Heights
Grandstand

Reserved Seating

The Lukey Heights Grandstand is located at the apex of the Circuit’s steep descent out of Turn 9, providing cross-Circuit views over arguably the track’s most challenging and technical section, all the way across to the Southern Loop.

Turn 9 is one of Phillip Island’s most notorious sections, with a long left-hander leading to a blind crest and a tricky downhill run into the Turn 10 hairpin, which is renowned as one of the Circuit’s prime overtaking locations.

The Lukey Heights Grandstand gives fans some of the best views of this fast-flowing and highly technical part of the Circuit and backs onto Lukey’s Lawn, one of the Circuit’s marquee off-track entertainment hubs.

Siberia
Grandstand

Reserved Seating

Perched on the eastern side of the Circuit and flanked to the left by the wild waters of Bass Strait, the Siberia Grandstand is a fan favourite and regarded by many as the ‘best seat in the house.’

The Grandstand’s location provides spectators unmatched views of the bikes negotiating the hairpin at Miller Corner (previously Turn 4), the sight of Jack Miller’s heartbreaking incident in 2022.

Watch as the riders click through their gears into Turn 5 before flashing through Siberia’s sweeping Turn 6 left-hander and roar up towards the northern part of the track.

The views across the southern half of the Circuit perfectly illustrate why Phillip Island is regarded as a racing paradise for most motorsport fans.

Nearby food, merchandise and bars mean you never have to travel far from your seat, and the super-screen opposite the Grandstand means you won’t miss a moment of the action.

Souvenir Tickets

If you're looking for a small piece of memorabilia to remember your Australian Motorcycle Grand Prix 2025 experience by, look no further than a limited-edition Souvenir Ticket.

Souvenir tickets are only made available for selected events and can be purchased through the normal purchase processes - be sure to select a 'Souvenir Ticket' ticket type when choosing your tickets. They incur a $15 surcharge.

Souvenir Tickets are a cherished keepsake and a lasting memento of the event. Souvenir Tickets are the same design as Collector Tickets, however they are mailed out after the event and do not include a lanyard or provide entry; instead, your mobile ticket will grant access on the day. Please note, Souvenir Tickets can only be purchased with an Australian postal address, so double-check that your Ticketmaster account details are up to date. Expected delivery - 4 weeks post event. Souvenir Tickets incur an additional $15 fee per ticket.

*Souvenir Tickets are not eligible for Resale.

What are Aisle Seat Tickets?

Looking to add some added comfort to your race day? We've got you covered with Aisle Seat Tickets!

Aisle Seat Tickets are available in all Grandstands, covering the first 4 seats from each aisle. They incur a $15 surcharge.
